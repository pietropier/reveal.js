Multi-Agent Coordinated Behaviors
       

Graph: \( \mathcal{G}(t) = ( \mathcal{V},\mathcal{E}(t)) \) represents the flow of information in the system at time \( t \)

\( \mathcal{V} \subset \mathbb{N} \) : set of agents

\( \mathcal{E} \in \mathcal{V} \times \mathcal{V}\) : set of interacting pairs

\( \mathcal{N}_i = \{ v_j \in \mathcal{V} \, | \, (v_i,v_j) \in \mathcal{E} \} \) : neighbors' set

Multi-Agent Coordinated Behaviors

Ensemble of \(N\) robots in a \(d\)-dimensional domain:

  • \(i^{\text{th}}\)-agent's state: \( x_i \in \mathbb{R}^d \)
  • system's state: \( x = [ x_1^T, \dots, x_N^T]^T \in \mathbb{R}^{dN} \)


Let's define:

  • edge potential : \( \quad \phi_{ij}( x_i-x_j )\succeq 0 : \mathbb{R}^{2d} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \)

  • total potential : \( \quad \Phi(x) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_\limits{i=1}^\limits{N} \sum _\limits{j \in \mathcal{N}_i}\phi_{ij}(x_i-x_j) \)

By choosing:

$$ \quad \dot{x}_i = - \frac{\partial \Phi(x)}{\partial x_i} = -\sum_\limits{j \in \mathcal{N}_i}\frac{\partial \phi_{ij}}{\partial x_i} \qquad \rightarrow \qquad $$

$$ \frac{d\Phi(x)}{dt} = -\| \frac{\partial \Phi(x)}{\partial x} \|^2 \leq 0 $$

Cort√©s, J., & Egerstedt, M. (2017). Coordinated Control of Multi-Robot Systems: A Survey. SICE Journal of Control, Measurement, and System Integration, 10(6), 495-503.
Consensus Equation

\( \quad \phi_{ij}(x_i,x_j) = \frac{1}{2}\| x_i-x_j \|^2 \)



The local descent flow: $$ \quad $$ $$ \dot{x}_i = -\sum_\limits{j \in \mathcal{N}_i}(x_i - x_j) $$ $$ \quad $$

Network requirement: \( \mathcal{G}(t) \) connected \( \forall t \geq 0 \)

Jadbabaie, A., Lin, J., & Morse, A. S. (2003). Coordination of groups of mobile autonomous agents using nearest neighbor rules. IEEE Transactions on automatic control, 48(6), 988-1001.
Formation Control

\( \quad \phi_{ij}(x_i,x_j) = \frac{1}{2}(\| x_i-x_j \| -\delta_{ij})^2 \)



The local descent flow: $$ \quad $$ $$ \dot{x}_i = -\sum_\limits{j \in \mathcal{N}_i}(\| x_i-x_j \| - \delta_{ij})(x_i - x_j) $$ $$ \quad $$

Network requirement: \( \mathcal{G}_F=(\mathcal{V},\mathcal{E}, [\delta_{ij}]_{i,j \in \mathcal{E}}) \) must be a rigid formation graph

Olfati-Saber, R., & Murray, R. M. (2002, December). Graph rigidity and distributed formation stabilization of multi-vehicle systems. In Decision and Control, 2002, Proceedings of the 41st IEEE Conference on (Vol. 3, pp. 2965-2971). IEEE.
Coverage control

Locational Cost: $$ \quad $$ $$ \mathcal{S}_{cov}(x) = \sum_{i=1}^N \int_{\mathcal{V}_i(x)} \| x_i-p \|^2 \, \phi(x) \, dp$$ $$ \quad $$

The local descent flow: $$ \quad $$ $$ \dot{x}_i = -\frac{\partial \mathcal{S}_{cov}(x)}{\partial x_i} = c_i(x) - x_i $$ $$ \quad $$ \( c_i(x) \) : center of mass of the \(i^{\text{th}}\) Voronoi cell

Network requirement: \( \mathcal{G}(t) \) must contain a Delaunay graph

Cortes, J., Martinez, S., Karatas, T., & Bullo, F. (2004). Coverage control for mobile sensing networks. IEEE Transactions on robotics and Automation, 20(2), 243-255.

Interaction model

\( \Delta\text{-disc} \) proximity graph: agents share information when their separation is less than \( \Delta \)

$$ \mathcal{E}(t) = \{(v_i,v_j) \in V \times V \, | \, \| x_i(t) - x_j(t)\| \leq \Delta, \, i,j = 1,\dots,N \} $$


Constraints

For the correct functioning of the sequence:

  • execution constraints - during execution of each controller
  • composition constraints - during transitions between two behaviors

Control Barrier Functions

Assume \(\mathcal{S} \subset D \subseteq \mathbb{R}^N \) a desired set for the state.
Define \( h(x): \mathbb{R}^N \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\), such that: $$ \quad $$ $$\mathcal{S} = \{ x \in \mathbb{R}^N \, | \, h(x) \geq 0 \} $$

Assume \(\alpha(\cdot)\) class-K function.
If \( h(0) \geq 0 \):

  • \( \dot{h}=-\alpha(h) \quad \Rightarrow \quad h(t) \searrow 0 \)

  • \( \dot{h}(x) \geq -\alpha(h(x)) \quad \Rightarrow \quad h(x(t)) \geq 0, \, \forall t \geq 0 \) (Comparison Lemma)

$$ \dot{h}(x) \geq -\alpha(h(x)) \quad \Rightarrow \quad h(x(t)) \geq 0, \, \forall t \geq 0 $$

Applying multi-agent system dynamics: \( \quad \dot{x} = f(x) + g(x)\,u \qquad x \in D \subseteq \mathbb{R}^N \quad u \in U \subseteq \mathbb{R}^m \)

$$ \dot{h}(x) = \frac{\partial h(x)}{\partial x} \dot{x} = \frac{\partial h(x)}{\partial x}(f(x)+g(x)\,u) = L_f h(x) + L_g h(x)\,u $$

Forward invariance of \( \mathcal{S} \) is certified by selecting a controller in: $$ \quad $$ $$ K(x) = \{ u \in U | L_g h(x)\,u + \alpha(h(x)) + L_f h(x) \geq 0\} \quad \forall x \in D $$

Thus, by choosing \( u(t) \in K(x): \)

  • \( x(0) \in \mathcal{S} \quad \Rightarrow \quad x(t) \in \mathcal{S}, \forall t \geq 0 \quad \) (forward invariance)
  • \( x(0) \notin \mathcal{S} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \lim_\limits{t \rightarrow \infty} x(t) \in \mathcal{S} \quad \) (robustness)
Xu, X., Tabuada, P., Grizzle, J. W., & Ames, A. D. (2015). Robustness of control barrier functions for safety critical control. IFAC-PapersOnLine, 48(27), 54-61.

Each behavior can be represented by a tuple: $$ \quad $$ $$ B_k = (\mathcal{U}_k, \, \bar{\mathcal{E}}_k, \, \Gamma_k) $$ $$ \quad $$

  • \( \mathcal{U}: \mathbb{R}^N \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^N \) coordinated control (e.g. \(-\frac{\partial \Phi(x)}{\partial x}\))
  • \(\Gamma \subset \mathbb{R}^N \) interrupt set
  • \( \bar{\mathcal{E}} \subseteq V \times V \) interaction structure required for \( \mathcal{U}\)

The entire mission: $$ \quad $$ $$ \sigma = \{ B_1,\dots,B_M \} $$ $$ \quad $$

Execution constraints

During execution of \( B_k \):

  1. Proximity maintenance: \( \quad \| x_i(t) - x_j(t)\| \leq \Delta \qquad \forall (i,j) \in \bar{\mathcal{E}}_k \)
  2. Collision avoidance: \( \quad \| x_i(t) - x_j(t)\| > \delta \qquad \forall i,j=1,\dots,N \)
  3. Obstacle avoidance: \( \quad \| x_i(t) - x_o(t)\| > \delta_o \qquad \forall i=1,\dots,N \, \text{and} \, o \in \text{"obstacles"} \)

We encode these constraints using the following barrier functions:

  1. \( h_{ij}^p := \Delta^2 - (x_i(t) - x_j(t))^T(x_i(t) - x_j(t)) \quad \forall (i,j)\in \bar{\mathcal{E}}_k \)
  2. \( h_{ij}^c := (x_i(t) - x_j(t))^T(x_i(t) - x_j(t)) - D_s^2 \quad \forall i > j \)
  3. \( h_{io}^o := (x_i(t) - x_o)^T P_o (x_i(t) - x_o) - 1 \quad \forall i \in V, \, o\,\in\,\text{"obstacles"} \)
Wang, L., Ames, A. D., & Egerstedt, M. (2016, December). Multi-objective compositions for collision-free connectivity maintenance in teams of mobile robots. In Decision and Control (CDC), 2016 IEEE 55th Conference on (pp. 2659-2664). IEEE.
Composition Constraints

In order for \( B_{k+1} \) to start: $$ \quad $$ $$ \bar{\mathcal{E}}_{k+1} \subseteq \mathcal{E}(t_s), $$ $$ \quad $$ where:

  • \( t_s \) activation time for \( B_{k+1} \)
  • \( t_s \, > \) \( B_k \) end-time

Idea: apply same proximity maintenance constraint: $$ \quad $$ $$ \| x_i(t) - x_j(t)\| \leq \Delta \qquad \forall (i,j) \in \bar{\mathcal{E}}_{k+1} $$ $$ \quad $$

Only asymptotic convergence!

For the correct switch between behaviors finite time convergence is needed

Consider a control barrier function h, with parameters \( \rho \in [0,1), \, \gamma > 0\) defined on \( D \), and initial state \(x_0\in D \setminus \mathcal{S} \).
Any Lipschitz continuous controller \( u \) in: $$ \quad $$ $$ K(x) = \{ u \in U | L_fh(x) + Lgh(x)\,u + \gamma \, sgn(h(x))\, |h(x)|^\rho \geq 0 \} $$ $$ \quad $$ drives \(x(t)\) to \(\mathcal{S}\) within finite time \( T = \frac{1}{\gamma(1-\rho)}h(x_0)^{1-\rho} \)

Li, A., Wang, L., Pierpaoli, P., & Egerstedt, M. (2018, October). Formally Correct Composition of Coordinated Behaviors Using Control Barrier Certificates. In 2018 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS) (pp. 3723-3729). IEEE.
Minimally Invasive Controller

Starting from a desired input \(\bar{u}\), we compute the minimally invasive certified input:

\begin{aligned} \min_{u\in U} & \| \, u - \bar{u} \, \|^2 & \\ \text{s.t.} & & \\ & L_g h_{ij}^p\,u \geq -\alpha^p(h_{ij}^p) - L_f h_{ij}^p \qquad &\forall \, (i,j) \in \bar{\mathcal{E}}_k \\ & L_g h_{ij}^c\,u \geq -\alpha^c(h_{ij}^c) - L_f h_{ij}^c \qquad &\forall \, i < j \in V \\ & L_g h_{ij}^o\,u \geq -\alpha^o(h_{ij}^o) - L_f h_{ij}^o \qquad &\forall \, i \in V \quad o \in \text{"obstacles"} \\ & L_g h_{ij}^p\,u \geq -\gamma \, sgn(h(x))\, |h(x)|^\rho - L_f h_{ij}^p &\forall \, (i,j) \in \bar{\mathcal{E}}_{k+1} \end{aligned}
